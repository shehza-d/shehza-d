# 📖 The Duʿā

**رَبِّ إِنِّي لِمَا أَنزَلْتَ إِلَيَّ مِنْ خَيْرٍ فَقِيرٌ**
(Surah Al-Qaṣaṣ, 28:24)

**Translation:**
“My Lord, indeed I am, for whatever good You would send down to me, in need.”

---

## 🕌 Context in the Qur’an

* After Prophet Mūsā (عليه السلام) fled from Egypt (escaping Pharaoh’s people), he arrived in **Madyan** tired, hungry, and without provisions.
* At the well of Madyan, he saw **two women** struggling to water their flock because other shepherds were blocking them.
* Out of compassion, he helped them water their animals.
* After doing this favor, he moved aside and sat in the shade, exhausted, without food or shelter.
* At that moment, he turned to Allah with this duʿā, admitting his **poverty, weakness, and total dependence** on Allah.

---

## 🌿 Lessons & Benefits

1. **Admitting Neediness to Allah** – The duʿā shows humility, as Mūsā (عليه السلام) didn’t demand but acknowledged his dependence.
2. **Helping Others Before Asking** – He first helped the women, then turned to Allah, teaching us to serve others even in hardship.
3. **Power of Sincerity** – His humble duʿā led to immediate blessings:

   * The women returned and invited him to their father (Prophet Shuʿayb, عليه السلام according to most Mufassirūn).
   * He was given food, shelter, safety, and eventually marriage to one of the daughters.
   * He also gained employment and stability after years of difficulty.

---

### 🌟 Usage Today

* This duʿā is often recited by people seeking **rizq (sustenance)**, **relief in hardship**, or **help from Allah** when they are in a state of need.
* It’s a short, powerful reminder that **all good is from Allah** and we are always dependent on Him.

Perfect 👍 Let’s dive into the **Tafsīr of Prophet Mūsā’s Duʿā** (Qasas 28:24):

---

## 📖 The Verse

**فَسَقَى لَهُمَا ثُمَّ تَوَلَّىٰ إِلَى الظِّلِّ فَقَالَ رَبِّ إِنِّي لِمَا أَنزَلْتَ إِلَيَّ مِنْ خَيْرٍ فَقِيرٌ**
(Surah Al-Qaṣaṣ 28:24)

**Meaning:**
*“So he watered (the flock) for them, then he turned back to the shade and said, ‘My Lord, indeed I am, for whatever good You send down to me, in need.’”*

---

## 📝 Tafsīr (Classical Exegesis)

### 1. **Ibn Kathīr**

* After helping the two women, Mūsā (عليه السلام) sat under the shade of a tree, exhausted, hungry, and alone.
* His duʿā expressed **deep humility** and acknowledgment of his **absolute need for Allah’s blessings**.
* Ibn Kathīr notes: Mūsā had **nothing of worldly provision** — no food, no home, no family — so he begged Allah for *any good* He might send down.

---

### 2. **Al-Qurṭubī**

* Qurṭubī highlights that Mūsā didn’t ask specifically for food, shelter, or wealth.
* Instead, he made a **general duʿā**, showing his **complete submission** to Allah’s will.
* The word **"faqīr" (فقير)** here means: utterly poor, dependent, and in need before Allah.
* This shows that even a Prophet, in times of distress, turns to Allah in humility.

---

### 3. **At-Ṭabarī**

* Ṭabarī narrates that after this duʿā, Allah immediately sent relief:

  * The women came back to call him to their father (identified as Shuʿayb عليه السلام by most scholars).
  * There, he found **food, safety, and later marriage**, which became the turning point in his life.
* Thus, the duʿā was **instantly answered** with multiple blessings.

---

## 🌿 Key Lessons

1. **Humility in Asking** – Mūsā didn’t dictate his needs but acknowledged that all good is from Allah.
2. **Service before Asking** – He helped the women before turning to Allah, teaching us to act selflessly.
3. **General Duʿā is Powerful** – Sometimes leaving it open-ended (“any good”) brings more barakah than asking for one specific thing.
4. **Allah’s Immediate Response** – This shows Allah’s mercy in providing not just one need (food) but multiple: safety, shelter, family, and stability.

---

✅ This duʿā is a **beautiful model for us today** when we feel helpless, jobless, or in need of sustenance, marriage, or relief.
